About

Wanna know more?

  • 2012 - 2016

    An Artistic Beginning

    Quinly Creative was born out of a love for art. After 6 long years of art school at three different institutions, Quinlan extended her art practise from doodling still life apple formations, to installation art projects at music festivals. Graduating with a fine arts degree becomes much more valuable than expected.

  • September 2016

    The Future is Friendly

    Feeling a drive to be more entrepreneurial in her life’s ambitions, Quinlan set out to discover the technical side of the world of tech.  After having attended a 3-month, high intensity coding bootcamp course in Vancouver, Quinlan obtains the necessary front-end development and design skills to work on one of the biggest websites on the internet.

  • 2017 - 2018

    Learning from Pros

    Landing her first job in one of the most visited websites in the world, Quinlan is mentored by some of Canada's best developers. For a year, she absorbs all of that juicy development knowledge and makes magic happen with a team of project managers, developers and designers.

  • September 2018 - Present

    Back to Basics

    Quinlan craves more art and image-making. She invents her own intensive learning course involving self-directed design and web projects, unpaid learning experience from mentors and friends, and a revisit to academia (CalArts) to study graphics and UX/UI in depth. With the help of her fierce freelancer friends, Quinly Creative is born.

Fondation Arbour

Website Resign, Squarespace Integration

View Live Project

Fondation Arbour's website was originally built on wordpress with a divi theme builder. The client was dissatisfied with the user experience associated with wordpress, so we decided to migrate them onto Squarespace instead. During the process, they asked for a more clean/modern design as well as some rebranding and content hierarchy assessment. This lead us to restructure their entire information architecture to create a better user experience for their site visitors.

  • April 2018
  • Client: Fondation Arbour
