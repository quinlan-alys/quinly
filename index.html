About

Wanna know more?

  • 2012 - 2016

    An Artistic Beginning

    All art all the time. Quinlan spends her days knee deep in Foucault, and wrapping herself in plastic wrap as a form of performance art. The young artist continues to explore the realm of the digital world. It is then when she discovers the art of web development, alongside interactive web art. A new door opens...

  • September 2016

    The Future is Friendly

    An ambitious university grad gets serious about tech. Attending a 3-month, high intensity coding bootcamp course in Vancouver, Quinlan obtains the necessary front-end devleopment and design skills to make it in the world of tech. And so she does...

  • 2017 - 2018

    Learning from the All-Stars

    Landing her first job in one of the most visited websites in the world, Quinlan is mentored by some of Canada's best developers. For a year, she absorbs all of that juicy development knowledge and makes magic happen with a team of project managers and designers.

  • September 2018 - Present

    Back to Basics

    Quinlan craves more art and image-making. She invents her own intensive learning course involving self-directed design and web projects, unpaid learning experience from mentors and friends, and a revisit to academia (CalArts) to study graphics and UX/UI in depth. Feeling ambitiously entrepreneurial all her life, now is the time. With the help of her fierce freelancer frienemies, Quinly Designs is born.

Avery Florence Website

Website Redesign / Rebrand

Avery Florence Website was a project involving a total rebrand and redeisgn of an existing wix site. The new site is built using a customized bootstrap template. The text and images are presented statically. However, the tour dates and contact form were implemented using third party APIs from Bandsintown and Mailchimp. It includes This one-page, mobile responsive, smooth scrolling website was completed in slightly under one week.

  • October 2018
  • Client: Avery Florence

Fondation Arbour

Website Redesign/Squarespace Integration

Fondation Arbour's website was originally built on wordpress with a divi theme builder. The client was dissatisfied with the user experience associated with wordpress, so we decided to migrate them onto Squarespace instead. During the process, they asked for a more clean/modern design as well as some rebranding and content hierarchy assessment. This lead us to restructure their entire information architecture to create a better user experience for their site visitors.

  • April 2018
  • Client: Fondation Arbour
